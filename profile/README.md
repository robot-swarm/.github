# 🤖 Robot Swarm

> Small autonomous robots that wander the hallways of Fontys ICT — like pets that just happen to live at school.

---

## What is this?

Robot Swarm is a student project at **Fontys ICT Delta** building small, autonomous robots that roam the corridors of the building on their own. They react to students and teachers, avoid obstacles, and return to their charging station when the battery runs low.

The goal is for the robots to feel alive — small, curious creatures rather than mechanical devices.

## Phase 1 Goals

Phase 1 is about learning fast — not delivering a finished robot. We build small prototypes, test them, see what breaks, and improve.

**Key questions we are answering:**
- What navigation approach works best in a busy corridor environment?
- What causes the robot to get stuck, and how do we prevent it?
- When and how should the robot return to its charging station?
- What is the simplest hardware setup good enough to build on in Phase 2?

**Phase 1 ends with:**
- A decision document covering the chosen navigation approach, hardware recommendation, and lessons learned
- At least one physical prototype with a short demo or video

## Approach

This project has been attempted seven times before — and failed every time. Previous teams tried to build everything at once, picked hardware too early, or got stuck on navigation and never recovered.

This time, we do it differently:

- **Start small.** Prove the basics before adding complexity.
- **Don't pick hardware until you know what you need.** Simulation and simple test robots come first.
- **Don't skip phases.** Each phase has a clear exit condition before the next one starts.

---

## Safety

Non-negotiable. The robots operate around people.

- Small and light — a collision causes no harm
- Slow enough that people can easily step out of the way
- Sensor-equipped to stop immediately if something is in the path
